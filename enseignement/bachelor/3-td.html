Exercise 1 : Signed number and Two's complement

  1. We consider a 16-bit representation of integer numbers. Give the internal representation of:
    • $(+ 1 032)_{10}$ in signed number and in Two's complement
    • $(- 721)_{10}$ in signed number and in Two's complement
  2. We consider a 16-bit representation of integer numbers. Give the decimal value of the following binary strings, successively considering that it is a signed value representation, then in Two's complement.
    • $(0101 0000 1100 1101)_2$
    • $(1010 0001 0011 0011)_2$

$(+ 1 032)_{10} = (0000~0100~0000~1000)_2$ (signed number and in Two's complement)
$(- 721)_{10} = (1000~0010~1101~0001)_2$ (signed number) or $(- 721)_{10} = (1111~1101~0010~1111)_2$ (Two's complement)

$(0101 0000 1100 1101)_2 = (20 685)_{10}$ (signed number and in Two's complement)
$(1010 0001 0011 0011)_2 = (- 8 499)_{10}$ (signed number)
$(1010 0001 0011 0011)_2 = (- 24 269)_{10}$ (Two's complement)
Exercise 2 : Fixed-point arithmetic

Perform the following conversions (with 256 as a fixed scaling factor):

  • $(1 432.45)_{10}$ in the binary representation (base $2$)
  • $(1 432.45)_{10}$ in the hexadecimal representation (base $16$)

$1432 \div 2 = 716$
$716 \div 2 = 358$
$358 \div 2 = 179$
$179 \div 2 = 89.5$
$89 \div 2 = 44.5$
$44 \div 2 = 22$
$22 \div 2 = 11$
$11 \div 2 = 5.5$
$5 \div 2 = 2.5$
$2 \div 2 = 1$
$1 \div 2 = 0.5 $
$(1432)_{10} = (101~1001~1000)_2 = (598)_{16}$

$0.45 \times 2=0.90; $
$0.90 \times 2=1.8; $
$0.8 \times 2=1.6; $
$0.6 \times 2=1.2; $
$0.2 \times 2=0.4; $
$0.4 \times 2=0.8;$
$0.8 \times 2=1.6; $
$0.6 \times 2=1.2; $
$0.45 = (0111 0011)_2 = (73)_{16}$
$(1 432.45)_{10} = (10110011000.01110011)_2 = (598.73)_{16}$
Exercise 3 : Floating-point arithmetic

  1. We consider a representation of floating point numbers according to the IEEE 754 binary32 standard. What is the internal representation of the following numbers?
    • $(1 432.45)_{10}$
    • $(- 721.25)_{10}$
  2. We consider a representation of floating point numbers according to the IEEE 754 binary32 standard. What is the decimal value of the following numbers?
    • $(1~~10001100~~10101110000000000000000)_2$
    • $(0~~10000101~~11011111001000100000000)_2$

$(1 432.45)_{10} = (10110011000.0111001100)_2 = 101100110000111001100 \times 2^{10}$
the exponent : $10 + 127 = 137 = (10001001)_2$
$(1 432.45)_{10} = (0~~10001001~~01100110000111001100000)_2$
